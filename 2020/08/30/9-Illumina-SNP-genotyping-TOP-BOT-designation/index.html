9. the TOP/BOT designation (Illumina SNP genotyping)

Posted on 2020-08-30 In technical Views:

“TOP/BOT” Strand and “A/B” Allele

Preface

Historically, strand designation and orientation have presented many challenges to researchers in the field of genetics when it comes to consistency and comparing results across platforms and organizations.

A method was developed to consistently designate SNPs based on the actual or contextual sequence of each individual SNP. The advantage is that it will consistently designate the same SNP orientation and allele calls even if public SNP databases and genome assemblies change.

This method was developed for microarray genotyping data. Though it is an era of NGS, it would be helpful to learn this method and the corresponding data format, when handling some microarray data.

Designation based on the actual polymorphism

The simplest case occurrs when one of the possible variation os the SNP is A (adenine), and the remaining variation is either C (cytosine) ot G (guanine). In this instance, the sequence is designated “TOP”, and the A nucleotide is designated Allele “A”, while the C or G is Allele “B”.

Similar to the rules of reverse complementarity, when one of the possible variation of the SNP is T (thymine), and the remaining variation if either C or G. Then the sequence for the SNP is designated “BOT” and the T nucleotide if designated Allele “A”. The C or G nucleotide is Allele “B”.

A condensed way of remembering these cases is the this method aims to designate the A as “TOP” and Allele “A”, and the T as Allele “A” on “BOT”. More examples in the following table.

Sequence Strand designation Allele “A” Allele “B”
NNN[G/T]NNN BOT T G
NNN[A/G]NNN TOP A G
NNN[C/T]NNN BOT T C
NNN[A/C]NNN TOP A C

Designation based on the surrounding sequence

If the SNP is an [A/T] or a [C/G], then the above rules do not apply. For the [A/T] SNP, the presence of an A would indicate “TOP” and the presence of a T would indicate “BOT”. But, both the A and the T would be designated as Allele “A” resulting in ambiguity.

A “sequence walking” technique is further employed.

For the walking method, the actual SNP is considered to the position “n”. The nucleotide immediately before and after the SNP are “n-1” and “n+1”, etc. Using this method, sequence walking continuous until an unambiguous pairing is present. In the instance of the following figure, this occurs at the “n-1|n+1” pairing (C/T).

To designate Strand, when A or T in the first unambiguous pair is on the 5’ side, then the sequence is designated “TOP”, vice versa (“BOT” when A/T on the 3’ side). To designate Allele for an [A/T] SNP, when the Strand is “TOP” then Allele “A”=A and Allele “B”=T. When the Stand is “BOT”, then Allele “A”=T and Allele “B”=A. For a [C/G] SNP, Allele “A”=C and Allele “B”=G when Strand is “TOP”. When the Strand is “BOT” then Allele “A”=G and Allele “B”=C.

More examples in the following table.

Sequence Strand designation Allele “A” Allele “B”
CAG[A/T]TAT BOT T A
AAT[C/G]AAG TOP C G
GAT[A/T]CAG TOP A T
AGG[A/T]TGA BOT T A

Consistent Strand and Allele designation

Sequence Strand designation Allele “A” Allele “B”
[G/A] TOP A G
[C/T] BOT T C
TGA[A/T]ACC TOP A T
GGT[A/T]TCA BOT T A
